Logic & Usage

This is a dependency graph with a schedule attached: if aligned ASI exists, what has to light first, what follows from it, and what stays hard regardless?

Use the map

  • Click a node to trace a focused dependency tree with directional arrowheads.
  • Click empty space to clear the trace.
  • Toggle layers in the lower-left legend to isolate one part of the system.
  • Turn on shadow mode to reveal dual-use risk paths.
  • Drag nodes to declutter. Drag the canvas to pan. Scroll or pinch to zoom.

Read the structure

  • Left to right is earliest maturity, not importance.
  • Metro-style edges are quiet by default; trace mode makes direction explicit.
  • Thicker edges are strong enablers; thinner edges are partial enablers; dashed edges are feedback or meaning links.
  • Speculative edges are hidden by default for readability. Use the legend toggle to show them.
  • Pulsing nodes are maintained states that decay without ongoing institutional work.
  • Double-ring nodes are irreducible sinks: governance, meaning, and great-power peace.

Takeaway

The crowded middle of the timeline, roughly 2032-2040, hinges on a few hubs: fusion, superconductivity, robotics, and nanotech. The graph also shows where aligned ASI assists but cannot substitute for institutions, purpose, and peace.
